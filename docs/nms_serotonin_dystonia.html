Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome, Serotonin Syndrome, and Acute Dystonia

1 Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome, Serotonin Syndrome, and Acute Dystonia

NEUROLEPTIC MALIGNANT SYNDROME (NMS)

Signs and symptoms

  1. Mental status change: agitated delirium, confusion, encephalopathy (82%)

  2. Muscular rigidity: “lead pipe” tremor (up to 92%) à elevated CPK and myoglobinuric acute renal failure

  3. Hyperthermia: >38°C (87%), >40°C** **(40%)

  4. Autonomic instability: tachycardia (88%), labile BP (77%), tachypnea (73%), diaphoresis

Diagnosis

1.* Exclude other etiologies: *acute drug intoxication, metabolic derangements, meningitis, etc.

Treatment

  1. Admit to ICU

  2. Discontinue offending agent** **(e.g. neuroleptics, D2 blocking antiemetics or abrupt w/d levodopa)

  3. Supportive care for Autonomic instability, DVT/PE prophylaxis, *IVFs to prevent **rhabdo*

  4. Symptomatic treatment:

  1. Agitation - Lorazepam 0.5-1mg prn (may also help with rigidity)

  2. Rigidity – Dantrolene 0.25-2mg/kg IV q6-12 hours (max dose 10mg/kg/day; be aware of hepatotoxicity)

  3. Fever – cooling blankets, ice lavage, acetaminophen

SEROTONIN SYNDROME (SS)

Signs and symptoms

1.* **Mental status change*: agitated delirium, restlessness, disorientation

2.* Neuromuscular hyperreactivi**ty*: tremor, rigidity, myoclonus, rigidity, hyperreflexia (+Babinski and clonus)

3.* **Autonomic instability*: dilated pupils, diaphoresis, hypertension, diarrhea, vomiting

Diagnosis

1.* Exclude other etiologies: *acute drug intoxication, metabolic derangements, meningitis, etc.

Treatment

  1. Admit to ICU

2.** **Discontinue offending agent (e.g. SSRIs, MAOIs, buspirone, trazodone, lithium, opiates, etc.)

  1. Supportive care for Autonomic instability, DVT/PE prophylaxis

  2. Symptomatic treatment:

    1. Agitation, Neuromuscular hyperreactivity - Lorazepam 0.5-1mg prn
  1. Cyptoheptadine (only for severe cases)** **12 mg PO, then 2mg q2 hours until clinical response

ACUTE DYSTONIC REACTION

Signs and symptoms

  1. *I**ntermittent or sustained dystonic posturing* due to blockade of D2 receptor (neuroleptics, certain antiemetics)

Diagnosis

  1. Review medication list/MAR for offending agent.

  2. Exclude metabolic derangements (hypomagnesemia, hypocalcemia) or other drug toxicity.

Treatment

  1. Discontinue offending agent

  2. Diphenhydramine 25-50mg IV/IM vs Lorazepam 0.5-1mg IV (acute); Diphenhydramine 25mg PO qid (x48hr)

  3. Alternative therapy: Benztropine 2mg IV/IM (acute); Benztropine 1-2mg PO bid (x48hr)
