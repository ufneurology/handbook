Subarachnoid hemorrhage

Author

Luis Silva

Modified

May 10, 2026

1 Differential diagnosis

Imaging pattern Differential
Thunderclap headache SAH, pituitary apoplexy, arterial dissection, DVST, hypertensive emergency, RCVS, intracranial hypotension, primary thunderclap headache
Sulcal subarachnoid hemorrhage RCVS, DVST, vasculitis, mycotic aneurysm, trauma, amyloid angiopathy, meningeal neoplasm, dural AV fistula, meningitis

2 Immediate management in the ED

2.1 Confirm the diagnosis

  • Non-contrast CT head
  • LP if CT findings are normal and suspicion remains high
    • Xanthochromia may be falsely negative if symptom onset was less than 6 hours ago

2.2 Management after confirmed SAH

  • Obtain CBC, electrolytes, Ca, Mg, PT/PTT, glucose
  • ECG and CXR to rule out neurogenic pulmonary edema or cardiac stunning
  • Keep SBP <160* mm Hg until aneurysm secured. Use labetalol 10 mg IV if HR >60, hydralazine 10 mg IV for HR <60. *(no exact goal beside <180 in AHA 2023 guidelines)
  • Neurosurgery consultation for DSA and/or EVD
  • EVD if patient has depressed LOC and hydrocephalus
  • Tranexamic acid 1 g IV if onset within past 72 hours; caution in patients with severe coronary artery disease or who have a delayed presentation and may already be in vasospasm
  • Monitor for complications: rebleeding, hydrocephalus, vasospasm and hyponatremia due to cerebral salt wasting or SIADH
  • Call NICU consultant, admit to ICU under Neurosurgery
  • Bedside dysphagia evaluation to be performed by nursing. NPO/SLP if fails
    • No oral meds prior to bedside dysphagia screen

3 Admission Orders

  1. Include WFNS, Modified Fisher, Hunt and Hess scores in initial note (See tables below)

  2. Admit to NICU unit 47 under neurosurgery service

  3. Neuro checks per unit routine

  4. Order STAT head CT if neurologic deterioration.

  5. Blood pressure management:

    • Labetalol 10 mg, every 15 minutes if HR >60, alternatively use hydralazine 10 mg for HR <60. For refractory HTN, initiate nicardipine infusion and titrate to effect.
    • Unsecured aneurysm, keep SBP <160 mm Hg*
    • Consider inserting arterial line for dynamic monitoring

  6. Continue TXA 1g q6h until aneurysm is secured (no more than 72h); caution in pts with severe cardiac disease

  7. Nimodipine 60 mg every 4 hours by mouth or NG tube for 21 days for delayed cerebral ischemia prophylaxis. Transition to 30 mg q2h if patient develops hypotension with typical dosing.

  8. Monitor for elevated intracranial pressure

  9. Discuss management with NCCM fellow and neurosurgery

  10. Strict bedrest and strict I/Os, insert urinary catheter

  11. Daily electrolytes to monitor for hyponatremia

  12. IV fluids: 0.9% normal saline to maintain euvolemia

  13. NPO for angiogram OR bedside swallow study and diet, formal swallow study if needed

  14. Continue necessary home medications; hold all antithrombotic medications

  15. Pain management: scheduled Tylenol/acetaminophen 1g q6h if no liver disease. Codeine 30-60 mg q 6 hours PRN OR Ultram (tramadol) 25-50 mg q 6 hours PRN IF patient does not have seizures

  16. In case of seizures: Keppra (levetiracetam). (try to avoid fosphenytoin - poorer outcomes)

  17. DVT prophylaxis: SCDs, no SC heparin

  18. GI prophylaxis: PPI (e.g., pantoprazole, omeprazole or lansoprazole [if gtube])

  19. Consult PMR/therapy services as indicated

  20. Code status

4 Grading scales

4.1 Modified Fisher Scale for vasospasm prediction

Score Description
0 no subarachnoid blood
1 thin focal or diffuse SAH; no intraventricular blood
2 thin focal or diffuse SAH; intraventricular extension
3 thick focal or diffuse SAH; no intraventricular blood
4 thick focal or diffuse SAH; intraventricular extension

4.2 World Federation of Neurological Surgeons

Grade Description
I GCS 15, no motor deficit
II GCS 13-14, no motor deficit
III GCS 13-14, motor deficit Present
IV GCS 7-12, motor deficit present or absent
V GCS 3-6, motor deficit present or absent

4.3 Hunt and Hess

Grade Description
0 unruptured aneurysm
1 asymptomatic or mild HA and nuchal rigidity
1a no meningeal signs but have fixed neurologic deficit
2 CN palsy, mod/severe HA, nuchal rigidity
3 mild focal deficit, confusion or drowsiness
4 stupor, mod/severe hemiparesis
5 deep coma, decerebrate posturing, moribund appearance
Note

For Hunt and Hess, consider adding one grade for severe vasospasm on angiogram or serious systemic disease.

5 Events and complications after aneurysmal SAH

Early Late Chronic
Rebleeding Cerebral vasospasm Chronic hydrocephalus that may require placement of a shunt
Acute hydrocephalus Delayed cerebral ischemia Behavior, personality, memory changes
Seizures Pituitary dysfunction Need for further retreatment for recurrent or residual aneurysm
Cardiopulmonary issues and stress induced cardiomyopathy Infection related to surgery or ventriculostomy
Sodium and electrolyte abnormalities
