MYASTHENIA GRAVIS EXACERBATION Epi: 2:3 M:F, bimodal peaks – female 20s, males 50s, 20% bulbar only, 2/100,000 annual Presentation: facial diparesis, ptosis, neck extensor weak, nasal voice, hypotonic/weak * may be precipitated by med non-compliance, excessive activity, infection, or drugs1 * diff from “cholinergic crisis” – weakness, fasciculations, inc sweating/salivation, and miosis Workup + Admission (similar respiratory concerns as acute GBS) 1) Assess respiratory status with vital capacity (VC) and negative inspiratory force (NIF), follow q2 hours if worrisome, intubate if VC < 15cc/kg & trending downward. Pulse ox and ABG changes will lag hypoventilation! 2) Consider use of Bipap/non-invasive ventilation before intubation in myasthenia crisis, as it can prevent intubation unless patient is hypercarbic Consider intubation: -20/30/40 rule: VC <20ml.kg (or 1 liter); MIP<30, MEP <40. Reduction in VC, MIP, MEP >30%, PO2 <70 mmHg on room air, PCO2 >50 mmHg w.acidosis Estimated VC = counting aloud in one breath x 100 cc (ex. counts to 25 = 2.5 liters). If facial weakness, VC may be lower due to poor seal on apparatus. If patient vented for >7 days, consider trach and PEG  Intubation: Etomidate 0.2-0.3mg/kg, non-depolarizing paralytics if necessary (vecuronium)  Extubation: A Rapid Shallow Breathing Index (RSBI) of 100 predicts a 95% chance of failure of extubation. This is calculated by dividing tidal volume by respiratory rate. Also FVC >15 mL/kg, NIF >30 cmH2O, and PEF >40 cm H2O 3) Check CXR and cultures 4) Admit to NICU 5) Eliminate any medications that can exacerbate myasthenic symptoms (see list below) 6) If new diagnosis: - check acetylcholine receptor antibodies - order EMG/NCV with repetitive stim, consider single fiber - consider tensilon test - check thyroid function tests - CXR +/- chest CT to rule out thymoma 7) Acute treatment with IVIg and/or plasma exchange, plus initiation of acetylcholinesterase inhibitors (below). Start steroids, however steroids may acutely worsen symptoms. 8) GI and DVT prophylaxis 9) Order PT/OT/Rehab consults – may need Rehab or SNF Acute and Chronic Treatment: 1) IVIg or plasma exchange – see GBS section for orders. 2) Start Mestinon 30 PO TID, increase to 60-120 mg q4-6 hrs (maintenance), can increase to 120 mg q3 hrs (maximum). Long acting Mestinon (timespan) can be given at night for pts with nocturnal or early morning weakness. IV Mestinon dose is 1/30th of oral Mestinon. Use Robinul for excessive GI cholinergic effects, but watch for ileus/megacolon. Hold if patient is ventilated (secretions) 3) Long-term Rx: Prednisone, to consider Imuran, other immune suppressants, thymectomy (RCT proven benefit in all patients with generalized myasthenia, AchR binding Ab positive). existing anticholinesterase medications should be stopped while patient’s on vent, due to increased secretions affecting weaning. Restart at ½ dose 1 day prior to extubation.
