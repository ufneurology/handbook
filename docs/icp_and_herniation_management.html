ICP and Herniation Management

  1. IV fluids: 0.9% normal saline to maintain euvolemia
  2. NPO for angiogram OR bedside swallow study and diet, formal swallow study if needed
  3. Continue necessary home medications; hold all antithrombotic medications
  4. Avoid Valsalva maneuver: use antiemetics, antitussives and stool softeners
  5. Pain management: scheduled Tylenol/acetaminophen 1g q6h if no liver disease. Codeine 30-60 mg q 6 hours PRN OR Ultram (tramadol) 25-50 mg q 6 hours PRN IF patient does not have seizures
  6. In case of seizures: Keppra (levetiracetam). (try to avoid fosphenytoin - poorer outcomes)
  7. DVT prophylaxis: SCDs, no heparin
  8. GI prophylaxis: PPI (e.g., pantoprazole, omeprazole or lansoprazole [if gtube])
  9. Consult PMR/Speech therapy as indicated
  10.
