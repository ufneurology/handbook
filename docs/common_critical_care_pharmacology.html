1 Common Critical Care Pharmacology

Common Critical Care Pharmacy

Pearls Adverse Reactions Dose Sedatives - Contraindications: Allergy to soy or egg - Use with caution in patients with cardiovascular disease Rapid onset and offset - Lipid vehicle is particularly susceptible to bacterial contamination, change IV tubing and bottle every 12 h - Blue-green discoloration of urine may occur - Propofol delivers 1.1 kcal/ml

  • Hypotension

  • Apnea

  • Movement

  • Pain at the injection site

  • Hypertriglyceridemia

  • Pancreatitis

  • Propofol-related infusion syndrome (PRIS): hyperkalemia, dysrhythmia, lipemia, metabolic acidosis, heart failure, ± rhabdomyolysis; Risk increased in young people with high doses (>70 mcg/kg/min) for prolonged duration (>48 hrs)

  • Max 50 mcg/kg/min Propofol GABAa receptor agonist

  • Has analgesic properties

  • Goal is patient arousable with stimulation and sedative state when stimulation removed

  • Does not require mechanical ventilation

  • Hypotension

  • Transient hypotension, severe bradycardia, sinus arrest during loading dose

  • Bradycardia

  • Maintenance infusion: 0.2-1.4 mcg/kg/h

  • Loading dose (1 mcg/kg) NOT recommended

Dexmedetomidine Alpha-2 agonist

  • IV contains propylene glycol, which can accumulate and cause metabolic acidosis

  • Precipitation is possible; an inline filter recommended for infusions

  • Hypotension

  • Respiratory depression

  • Drowsiness

  • Pain at injection site

  • Akathisia

  • Confusion

  • Anterograde amnesia

  • Visual disturbances

  • Paradoxical reactions – hyperactivity and aggressive behavior

  • Loading dose: 0.02-0.04 mg/kg

  • Intermittent dose: 0.02-0.06 mg/kg q2-6 h (max 2 mg IV for agitation at 2 mg/min)

  • Maintenance infusion: 0.01-0.1 mg/kg/h

Lorazepam GABAa receptor agonist

  • Rapid onset and short duration

  • Active metabolites which may accumulate in renal dysfunction

  • Similar to lorazepam

  • Loading dose: 0.01-0.05 mg/kg

  • Maintenance infusion: 0.01-0.1 mg/kg/h

Midazolam GABAa receptor agonist

Pearls Adverse Reactions Usual dose Opioids - May be alternative in patients with morphine allergy - Do not use transdermal formulation in acute setting due to delayed onset (6–24 h)

  • Constipation

  • Miosis

  • Chest wall rigidity

  • Diaphoresis

  • Myoclonus

  • Respiratory depression

  • Bradycardia

  • Edema

  • Confusion

  • Sedation

  • Mood changes

  • Bolus: 50-100 mcg or 1-2 mcg/kg IVP

  • Maintenance: 0.7-10 mcg/kg/hr or 25-700 mcg/h

Fentanyl

  • May be alternative in patients with morphine allergy

  • Mood changes

  • Constipation

  • Miosis

  • Diaphoresis

  • Myoclonus

  • Respiratory depression

  • Bradycardia

  • Edema

  • Confusion

  • Sedation

  • Oral: 2-4 mg every 4-6 h

  • IV: 0.2-1 mg every 4-6 h Hydromorphone

  • Active metabolites may accumulate in renal dysfunction; reduced dose recommended

  • Can induce a histamine release that causes itching

  • Pruritus

  • Urinary retention

  • Pain at injection site

  • Dizziness

  • Fever

  • Myoclonus

  • May elevate ICP due to hypercarbia

  • Itching

  • Respiratory depression

  • Sedation

  • Hypotension

  • Mood changes

  • Xerostomia

  • Constipation

  • Bolus: 2-10 mg IVP

  • Intermediate dose: 2-8 mg q3-4 h

  • Maintenance infusion: 0.8-30 mg/h

  • Pediatric: 0.1-0.2 mg/kg IV over 5 min; maintenance infusion 0.02-0.03 mg/kg/h, titrate as needed

Morphine

  • Due to short half-life, effects may wear off before opioid is cleared from the system, resulting in renarcotization

  • Diaphoresis

  • Dyspnea

  • Withdrawal symptoms

  • Tachycardia

  • Hypertension

  • Nausea, vomiting

  • Tremor

  • IV/IM: 0.04-0.4 mg

  • Intranasal: 1-2 mg per nare into both nares, can be repeated q2-3 min and switch to IV/IM when possible

Naloxone (antagonist)

  • Cannot be reversed
  • May cause slight increases in ICP

Succinylcholine

  • No significant cardiovascular effects
  • No effect on ICP Intermediate 35-45 mins (up to 60 mins)

Prolonged 3-9 mins Faster onset with higher dose (2 min)

Adults and pediatrics: 0.1 mg/kg (up to 0.2 mg/kg) IV

Vecuronium

  • Longer half life in elderly
  • Elimination via enzymatic breakdown and does not rely on renal or liver function for clearance

Prolonged Adults: 45-75 mins Pediatrics: 20-35 mins

Fast 1.5-2 mins Adults and pediatrics: 0.15 mg/kg (up to 0.2 mg/kg) IV

  • Prolonged duration in renal failure Intermediate 20-35 mins (up to 60 mins)

Rapid 1-2 mins Faster onset with higher dose

Adults: 0.6 mg/kg (up to 1.2 mg/kg) IV Pediatrics: 0.45-0.6 mg/kg IV
