1 Anticoagulation Reversal

UF Anticoagulant reversal for intracranial hemorrhage (ICH) and other life threatening bleeding (2020)

Anticoagulant Reversal Comments Warfarin Vitamin K 10 mg IV over 15-30 min AND Prothrombin Complex Concentrates (PCC, eg. KCentra) 25 units/kg if INR 1.7-4, 35 units/kg if INR 4-6, or 50 u/kg if INR>6)

FFP is not recommended with use of PCC unless otherwise indicated Consider FEIBA in patients with a documented heparin allergy as PCCs contain a small amount of heparin - 500 units for INR ≤5 - 1000 units for INR >5 Un- fractionated heparin (UFH)

  • 1mg Protamine iv for each 100 units of UFH. Need to consider timing and total amount of UH administered over the last 3 hours in calculation (see next column).
  • Infusion should not exceed 5mg/minute due to risk of anaphylaxis.
  • Maximum single dose of Protamine is 50 mg, but can be repeated

Following a single bolus of heparin: a. Immediately: Dose 1mg protamine IV for every 100 units UFH b. 30 min to 1 hour after a bolus: Dose 0.5 mg protamine IV for every 100 units UFH. c. >2 hours after a bolus – Dose 0.25-0.375 mg protamine IV for every 100 units UFH For continuous infusion of heparin: Calculate dose (1 mg protamine per 100 units UFH) for all UFH given within the last hour + one half the dose of heparin given during the preceding hour + one-quarter of the dose of heparin given in the hour prior to that.

Low molecular weight heparin (LMWH)

  • 1 mg Protamine IV for each 1mg of Enoxaparin in last 8 hours

  • 1 mg Protamine IV for each 100 units of Dalteparin or Tinazeparin in last 8 hours

  • For either regimen, may consider Factor VIIa 45-90 mcg/kg IV for life threatening intractable bleed** (Round rVIIa dose to vial size)

  • Protamine only partially reverses the activity of LMWH.

  • Note differences in dosing between enoxaparin and other LMWHs

  • May administer 0.5 mg protamine for every 1 mg enoxaparin (or 100 units of dalteparin) if LMWH dose administered 8-12 hours prior

  • Round Factor VIIa dose to nearest 1 mg. Factor Xa inhibitors (Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Edoxaban)

Note: Andexanet alfa not FDA approved for Edoxaban reversal; requires attending approval to order

Andexanet alfa:** - Low Dose (Rivaroxaban 10 mg or less, Apixaban 5 mg or less) (Xa inhibitor given >8h prior or dose < than 10mg): 400mg bolus at 30mg/min followed by immediate infusion 4mg/min for 120 min (480mg total) - High Dose (Rivaroxaban greater than 10 mg, Apixaban greater than 5 mg) (Xa inhibitor given <8h prior or dose >10mg or dose or time unknown): 800mg bolus at 30mg/min followed by immediate infusion 8mg/min for 120 min (960mg total)

  • Send stat anti-Xa level (LMWH). Recs may alter depending on level
  • Reversal indicated if UFH level >0.6
  • Alternative if andexanet not available: PCC 50 IU/kg iv** or activated charcoal if Xa inhibitor given <8h prior
  • If no response to PCC or heparin allergy: -Factor VIIa 45-90 mcg/kg or FEIBA 50 units/kg may be tried if no response to PCC
  • Note: If anti-Xa level is <0.6 there is likely little anticoagulant effect and ANY “reversal” is not necessary

Dabigatran - Activated oral charcoal if ingestion occurred within 2 hours - Idarucizumab 5 gm IV once

  • Consider HD, especially if renal impairment
  • If no clinical response to idarucizumab, consider Prothrombin Complex Concentrates (PCC) 50 units/kg** OR FEIBA 50 units/kg may be tried if no clinical response to PCC or documented heparin allergy
  • Note: If thrombin time and aPTT are normal there is likely no anticoagulant effect and “reversal” is not necessary
