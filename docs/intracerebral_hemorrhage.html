Intracerebral Hemorrhage

INTRACRANIAL HEMORRHAGE Intraparenchymal hemorrhage  Hypertension most common etiology – subcortical/brainstem  Cerebral amyloid angiopathy (cortical)  Arteriovenous malformation  Neoplasm (primary central nervous system, metastatic, leukemia)  Hematologic (sickle-cell disease, neoplasm, thrombocytopenia) Moyamoya disease  Drug use (warfarin, DOACs, amphetamines, cocaine, phenypropanolamine)  Iatrogenic (peri-procedural) Subarachnoid hemorrhage  Basilar cisterns: cerebral aneurysm until proven otherwise, differential includes trauma, other vascular malformations  Isolated high convexity: trauma, cerebral amyloid angiopathy, RCVS ICH Volume estimation = (A x B x C)/2 A = Diameter, B = Diameter perpendicular to A C = Number of slices on CT multiplied by slice thickness in cm ICH scores and 30-day mortality 0 points: 0% 1 point: 13% 2 points: 26% 3 points: 72% 4 points: 97% 5 points: 100% 6 points: 100% Control BP: Goal SBP 100-140  Use labetalol 10-20 mg IVP q1h PRN or nicardipine drip (start a 5 mg/h and titrate up as needed). If a nicardipine drip is started, ask ED resident/attending to place arterial line. A PIV can only be used for 24h.  The emergency room is busy. Do not rely on the nurse or ED resident to monitor the blood pressure. The neurology resident must alert the staff if SBP>140. Additional guidelines and workup:  ABCs, intubation if GCS<8  HOB > 30°  Reverse anticoagulation, if applicable (reversal protocol chart). Hold antithrombotics.  Discuss admission disposition with attending/fellow. o Neurology generally admits intraparenchymal hemorrhage unless neurosurgery is planned. o Subarachnoid hemorrhages go to neurosurgery unless clearly non-aneurysmal.  Admit to NICU using “STROKE PROGRAM: ICH ADMISSION ORDERS IP UF” order set  On admission, place SCD for DVT prophylaxis. Re-start heparin/LMWH after 24h or stable repeat imaging.  MRI brain (+/- contrast) unless obvious hypertensive etiology  CTA head vs. MRA head vs. conventional angiogram (unless hypertensive) – discuss with attending/fellow  Consider UDS, also blood cultures/TEE if concern for endocarditis
